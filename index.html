ALBERT EINSTEIN
ABOUT ME


Albert Einstein, a physicist and thinker devoted to unraveling the mysteries of the universe. His life's work has been a journey through the realm of theoretical physics, seeking to illuminate the fundamental principles governing our cosmos.
His work continues to inspire and guide scientists, scholars, and thinkers worldwide.Einstein believes that imagination is more important than knowledge and that curiosity knows no bounds.
His dedication to the pursuit of truth has driven to explore the mysteries of the universe relentlessly, transcending the conventional boundaries of science and philosophy.

EXPERIENCE
  • Worked as Technical Assistant in the Swiss Patent Office also appointed as Privatdozent in Berne

  • Professor of Theoretical Physics at Princeton

  • He contributed to statistical mechanics by his development of the quantum theory of a monatomic gas

  • He accomplished valuable work in connection with atomic transition probabilities and relativistic cosmology
EDUCATION

  • University of Zurich (PHD) : 1900 - 1905

  • Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich (Polytechnic) : 1896 - 1900

  • Aargau Cantonal School in Aarau (Secondary Schooling) : 1895 - 1896

  • Luitpold Gymnasium in Munich (Secondary schooling) : 1888 - 1894
ACHIEVEMENTS
  • Einstein showed that absolute time had to be replaced by a new absolute: the speed of light. Einstein went against the grain and totally dismissed the "Old Physics." He envisioned a world where space and time are relative and the speed of light is absolute.

  • He asserted the equivalence of mass and energy, which would lead to the famous formula E=mc^2

  • Einstein won the Nobel Prize in 1921.

  • Received Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society

  • The most prestigious award of the royal society ,Copley award was given for his outstanding Achievements in field of science
